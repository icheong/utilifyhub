What is an Aspect Ratio?

An aspect ratio represents the proportional relationship between the width and height of an image, video container, or monitor screen. Written as two values separated by a colon (e.g. Width:Height), it indicates scale dimensions regardless of overall resolution.

Common Aspect Ratios & Use Cases

  • 16:9 (High Definition): The international standard for HDTVs and YouTube. Common resolutions include 1920x1080 (FHD) and 3840x2160 (4K).
  • 9:16 (Vertical HD): Used across TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts.
  • 4:3 (Academy Standard): The vintage TV standard preceding HD formats.
  • 1:1 (Square Format): Popularized by Instagram posts and profile avatars.
  • 21:9 (Ultrawide): Common in gaming monitors and cinematic projections.

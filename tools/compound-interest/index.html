What is Compound Interest?

Compound interest calculates returns on your initial principal plus every dollar of accumulated interest earned in preceding periods.

The Formula

A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt) + PMT * [((1 + r/n)^(nt) - 1) / (r/n)]

Where A = final balance, P = principal, r = annual rate, n = compounding frequency, t = years, PMT = monthly deposit.

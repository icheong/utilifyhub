What is Compound Interest?

Compound interest has often been referenced as the "eighth wonder of the world". Unlike simple interest, which computes returns strictly on the initial principal capital, compound interest calculates interest on your initial principal **plus every dollar of accumulated interest** earned in preceding periods.

The Mathematics of Compounding Growth

The standard formula mapping compound interest with recurrent periodic deposits is:

A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt) + PMT * [((1 + r/n)^(nt) - 1) / (r/n)] * (1 + r/n)

Where:

  • A: The final future total balance of the investment.
  • P: The initial principal capital balance ($).
  • r: The annual nominal interest rate in decimal form (e.g., 7.5% = 0.075).
  • n: The compounding frequency per year (e.g., 12 for Monthly compounding).
  • t: The overall period of investment measured in years.
  • PMT: The periodic deposit contribution made at the start of each interval ($).

Maximizing Compounding Yields

Three core variables dictate the speed of your investment compounding:

  1. Time: The longer your savings remain invested, the faster the exponent curves compound interest upward.
  2. Compounding Frequency: Daily or monthly compounding yields higher overall returns than quarterly or yearly compounding because interest is reinvested faster.
  3. Regular Contributions: Adding regular monthly deposits exponentially expands your interest-generating foundation.

UUID, ULID, and Unique Identifier Standards

Globally unique identifiers are fundamental building blocks of distributed software systems. When multiple services or databases need to create records independently without central coordination, unique ID schemes prevent collisions without a shared counter or sequence.

UUID v4

UUID v4 consists of 128 random bits formatted as 32 hexadecimal digits: xxxxxxxx-xxxx-4xxx-yxxx-xxxxxxxxxxxx. The probability of collision between two random v4 UUIDs is approximately 1 in 5.3 × 10³⁶.

ULID

ULIDs encode a 48-bit millisecond timestamp followed by 80 random bits. Unlike UUIDs, ULIDs sort chronologically, making them ideal for database primary keys where insertion order matters.
